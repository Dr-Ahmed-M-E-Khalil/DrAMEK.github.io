Professional Profile

Dr Ahmed Khalil is a dedicated academic researcher and educator with over a decade of expertise in chemical, environmental, and materials engineering, particularly in nanotechnology, sustainable composite materials, water and wastewater treatment, and environmental remediation, among several chemical engineering applications. He holds a PhD in Chemical and Environmental Engineering from Kyushu University, Japan, and has built a distinguished career in chemical engineering, materials science, and environmental applications. His work spans (nano)materials, ceramic composites, functional material synthesis, algorithms, modelling, and simulations, contributing to over 30 peer-reviewed publications in high-impact journals, with over 1,700 citations.

Dr Khalil has participated in more than 24 international conferences, earning multiple best presenter awards. He excels at project management, advanced characterisation techniques, modelling and simulation, and the use of computational tools. He worked on a three-year independent research programme at the University of Exeter as part of the NERC-funded Fate and co-led its work package on the treatment of Emerging Contaminants in the Fate and Management of Emerging Contaminants (FaME) project using novel treatment graphene-based materials and treatment solutions and played a pivotal role in the EPSRC-funded Transforming the Foundation Industries Research and Innovation Hub (TransFIRe) programme, helping to shape advancements in UK industry.

In addition to his research, Dr Khalil contributes to academic development as an Associate Fellow of the Higher Education Academy. With international teaching experience across multiple universities (University of Exeter, UK; Kyushu University, Japan; and Cairo University, Egypt), he has supervised over 10 undergraduate and postgraduate students and taken a leading role in overseeing the Ceramics, Catalysis, and Functional Materials Laboratory at the University of Exeter. Passionate about inspiring future generations and boosting chemical and materials engineering research, he remains committed to advancing sustainable engineering practices and delivering innovative solutions.

Thesis Title: Applications and Development of Stochastic Global Optimization Methods for Thermodynamic Problems

Supervisors: MIT PhD and MSc holder, Professor Seif-Eddeen Khalid Fateen, Professor Mai Kamal Fouad

Thesis Overview: This project addressed phase stability and equilibrium in reactive and non-reactive thermodynamic systems using advanced stochastic swarm algorithms. Three algorithms—Monkey Algorithm (MA), Gravitational Search Algorithm (GSA), and Krill Herd Algorithm (KHA)—were evaluated, with KHA emerging as the most reliable. A hybrid algorithm (MAKHA), combining MA and KHA elements, demonstrated superior performance in solving complex thermodynamic benchmarks. This innovative approach highlighted the potential of MAKHA for process engineering design. More information here.
